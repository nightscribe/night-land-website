. . . . the last giant arrived . . .

. . . . limping from the South through the dark plain, leaving behind empty shells from the Hive. He saw in front of him the peak of grey metal through a sea of grey dunes.
 

He whispered with hatred the old call:
 

"Mother!"

 

 

He felt a tremor in the Earth, and a glowing dome rose out of the ground to feed him. For a few moments, he immersed himself gratefully in the pleasure of feeling his energy recovering again, and knowing that, after all, he'd live. Then, he looked forward, and he made a promise, to himself, to his brothers . .

. . . to the Hive.
