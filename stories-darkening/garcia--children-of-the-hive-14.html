But he realised in dismay that if he followed that direction again he could come to death. Dying, suddenly, was a real possibility, and not the creation of the Hive's maddened mind. It would be a strange irony of destiny if he, created to survive, died finally at the hands of his death-fearing mother. No, he couldn't die. Surviving was the main reason for his existence. He would survive.

The giant stood up carefully and he started to walk, sore and hungry, the first weary steps of a dangerous journey towards the light.

The giant wasn't used to the lack of stimulus, and he soon discovered that, if he didn't want to fall asleep again without noticing, he had to talk constantly to his brothers. Luckily, they had a lot to talk about. While he slept, his four brothers completed their journey and they had located again the Hive, in the deepest part of the Valley.
