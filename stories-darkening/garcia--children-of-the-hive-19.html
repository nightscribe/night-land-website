. . . for the new plan of the giants. Because they could only survive the Hive by being shrewder, and that meant being prepared for any eventuality. All the beings with some intelligence that lived outside the Pyramid were now linked in a net of freely shared information, and the giants were the only ones who could keep the integrity of the net. Whatever the Hive did, they were all ready.

The results were soon felt. The Hive's attacks to the outside world became less and less frequent. For the first time, semi-intelligent beings felt the yoke of fear was lifting slightly, and the difficult balance between terror and hunger wasn't so precarious any more. The giants started to see the situation as a war of attrition. The Earth-current wouldn't take long to run out, at the rate the Hive was consuming it. If they managed to keep the watch uninterruptedly till then, and the Hive didn't think a way of attacking that they couldn't hold back, they would be saved.

And at last . . .
