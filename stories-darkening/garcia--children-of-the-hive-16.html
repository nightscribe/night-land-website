His calls didn't get any answer from the Pyramid; however, something did hear them: an old device, without a doubt created by the Hive anticipating the return of the giants. It responded to his hunger and surrounded him with torches fed by the Earth-current, more than enough to calm his appetite.

The giants felt their hope revive: the Hive hadn't forgotten them completely. At least, not until after she arrived at the bottom of the Valley.

Full of energy, the second giant discovered he could speed up his metabolism to the point of being able to communicate with some of the longest-lived semi-intelligent beings, though he was never capable of identifying them visually.

The giants learned some strange things from them. The Hive seemed to enjoy a kind of cruel game, creating beings to try later to destroy them with all her strength. The only way to beat her in her own macabre game was to predict her movements, and therefore, those lesser children of the Hive had learned to extract information from her repositories: living human brains. But the Hive, after discovering their new skill, had started to kill her cells before they were captured.

A terrible possibility started to take shape in the mind of the giants. The Hive was mad. She could have forgotten them, but she could also be lying in wait for them, practising a strange kind of big game hunting with her own creatures.

What to do?
